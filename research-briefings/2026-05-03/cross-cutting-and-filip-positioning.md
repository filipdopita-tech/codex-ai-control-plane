## SECTION 1: AI × FINANCE OVERLAP — DETAILED MAPPING

### Current Landscape (2026)

**Robo-Advisor Evolution**: Modern robo-advisors (Vanguard Digital Advisor, Charles Schwab Intelligent Portfolios, Interactive Brokers TWS + AI) integrate LLM-based reasoning over prospectus data, earnings transcripts, and regulatory filings. ArXiv papers (2024-2026) document:
- **"Agreement-Gated Stress Testing of LLM-Judged Investment Rationales"** (ValueAlpha pattern): LLMs evaluate investment thesis before market confirmation, reducing retail investor overconfidence bias.
- **"Constrained LLM Agents in Cryptocurrency Markets"**: Trading hypothesis generation + factor identification via structured agents, with compliance guardrails.

**Fraud Detection AI**: Banking sector deploys ML/AI for real-time transaction monitoring (Actimize, Feedzai, Ayasdi), anomaly detection, and AML screening. EU DORA framework (Digital Operational Resilience Act, live 2025) mandates:
- AI risk governance for critical financial functions
- Third-party audit trails for algorithmic decision-making
- Incident reporting for AI-driven false positives

### EU AI Act High-Risk Classification (2026 Implementation)

**High-Risk Categories (Article 6, Annex III)**:
- **Credit scoring/lending decisions** → "May cause harm to fundamental rights"
- **Fraud detection in payments** → "Biometric verification, transaction monitoring"
- **AML customer screening** → "May produce unlawful discrimination"

**Enforcement Timeline**:
- May 2025: Act effective, forbearance period begins (non-compliance advisory only)
- May 2026: **HARD ENFORCEMENT** — fines up to 6% of annual turnover for high-risk violations
- First cases expected: Q2-Q4 2026 (credit scoring non-compliance, fraud detection bias)

## SECTION 5: CROSS-CUTTING RISKS — WHERE BUSINESSES CAN HARM EACH OTHER

### Risk 1: Scholarly Tone vs. Sales Energy (Brand Collision)

**Scenario**: 
- CIAD publishes AI governance framework flagging "risks of aggressive sales practices in fintech"
- OneFlow simultaneously launches aggressive B2B outreach campaign targeting institutional investors
- Media juxtaposes: "CIAD warns against aggressive fintech sales, but co-founder Filip's company uses those exact tactics"

**Mitigation**:
- Establish clear separation: CIAD publishes *neutral research*, not industry critiques
- OneFlow messaging emphasizes *responsible growth* (aligns with CIAD positioning, not in conflict)
- Have unified brand narrative: "OneFlow practices responsible AI because CIAD advises us"

### Risk 2: Regulatory Exposure (OneFlow Gets Caught in CIAD Critique)

**Scenario**:
- CIAD publishes critical analysis: "Current EU AI Act enforcement is ineffective because [X gap]"
- Regulators interpret this as: "CIAD is saying our enforcement is weak" → scrutiny of CIAD board/funding
- OneFlow gets collateral regulatory attention due to Filip's dual role

**Mitigation**:
- CIAD publications are *constructive criticism* (offer solutions, not complaints)
- When CIAD flags regulatory gaps, propose how OneFlow has solved them internally (shows leadership, not critique)
- Maintain separate legal governance (CIAD board ≠ OneFlow board, independent advisory board for CIAD)

### Risk 3: Capital Reallocation (OneFlow Investors Want CIAD Leverage)

**Scenario**:
- OneFlow closes institutional investors who expect CIAD = "free marketing arm" or "internal consultancy"
- Investors demand Filip allocate more time to CIAD-funded OneFlow initiatives
- CIAD's independence erodes; research becomes captive to OneFlow business interests

**Mitigation**:
- Set explicit governance rule: CIAD is *operationally independent*, no OneFlow revenue dependency
- Diversify CIAD funding (government grants, EU Horizon grants, donor base beyond OneFlow investors)
- Publish conflict-of-interest statement in CIAD research (transparency)

### Risk 4: Hiring Conflict (Talent War Between Organizations)

**Scenario**:
- Top AI researcher gets offers: CIAD (lower pay, research freedom) vs. OneFlow (higher pay, product pressure)
- OneFlow hires away all top talent from CIAD because it can outbid
- CIAD becomes "junior org under OneFlow's shadow"

**Mitigation**:
- Offer complementary career paths (CIAD = research focus, OneFlow = product impact)
- Create exchange programs (OneFlow engineers spend 10% time on CIAD research, CIAD researchers spend 10% on OneFlow product review)
- Sell CIAD work as "career stepping stone" (publish papers, build reputation, advance to OneFlow or elsewhere)

### Risk 5: Reputational Spillover (Scandal in One Harms Both)

**Scenario**:
- OneFlow faces customer dispute/scandal (alleged overcharging, bad faith deal terms)
- Media connects Filip to CIAD, questions credibility of CIAD governance research
- "How can we trust your AI governance advice when your company doesn't follow ethical practices?"

**Mitigation**:
- Maintain highest operational standards in OneFlow (customer disputes handled transparently, published case studies)
- CIAD publishes *external* case studies (not just OneFlow), builds reputation independent of OneFlow practices
- Establish external advisory board for CIAD (academic + regulatory credibility independent of Filip)
